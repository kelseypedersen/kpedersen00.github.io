My Thinking Style and Learning
March 15th, 2015

How you can use your understanding of your thinking style to your advantage at DBC to help you learn?

I am a concreate random learning style. This means that I like experimenting to find answers, taking risks, using my intuition and solving problems independently. I learn best when I am able to use trial-and-error approaches, able to compete with others and given the opportunity to work through the problems by myself. It's hard on me when I have restrictions and limitations, formal reports, choosing only one answer and having no options.

I agree with these learning style generalizations for the most part. I left out the part that I have a hard tim with routines, re-doing anything once it's done and keeping detailed records since I think those are three of my greatest strengths so doesn't fit into my personal learning style.

I can use my understanding of my learning style to understand that I like to have enough time on the exercises to be able to experiment with the answer. Even once I have found one way to pass the tests, I like to find one or two other ways to complete the same exercise. It's important to give myself time to do this.

What has been the biggest struggle for you?

The biggest struggle has been time management. I am working full-time during phase 0, which has been a good test in managing the time in my day. My commute is an hour each way so I work on the exercises in the morning and at night while on my commute.

Is it related to the way you think and learn or is it related to something else?

I think anyone in my situation would be feeling a bit overwhelmed in terms of managing both. I also like to feel like I have enough time on the exercises and do not want to have to rush. For the most part though, I think I do a good job of giving myself enough time at night and on the weekend to successfully complete the exercises.

If it's related to your learning style, start thinking of strategies to help you. Put some plans of action in place.

My new goal is to finish half the course work by Wednesday each week, so I feel like I have time at the end to go back and check my work and go through answers that I did not fully understand and want to review. I also need to focus on smaller steps, focus on specific tasks per day, rather than thinking I need to complete the entire weeks worth of work in one or two days. Overall, I think I'm doing a great job in managing all my obligations and learning a ton already!
